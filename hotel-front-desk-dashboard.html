Policy reference

Seven controlled Aurelia Grand Hotel Group documents, condensed for quick reference during a complaint or compensation decision.

AGHG-BGE-007

Brand Standards & Guest Experience Manual

Tone, ownership, and service language standards · v3.0

Aurelia service is calm, precise, personal, and accountable — the hotel owns what goes wrong rather than asking the guest to manage departments. Responses should name the issue, make one clear promise, and avoid over-apologizing without action.

Instead ofUse
“We apologize for any inconvenience.”“I am sorry the air-conditioning issue disrupted your rest.”
“That is our policy.”“Here is what I can do now, and what requires manager approval.”
“There is nothing else we can do.”“I have reviewed the available options and this is the approved resolution.”
AGHG-FDC-005

Front Desk Complaint Handling Playbook

Intake, triage, escalation, and closure · v3.0

A complaint is an opportunity to correct the stay, not an interruption. Assign an owner before handing a guest to another department, and never promise a remedy before reviewing records unless an immediate goodwill gesture is authorized.

SituationResponse target
In-house, urgent issueAcknowledge within 10 minutes
In-house, non-urgent issueAcknowledge within 30 minutes
Post-stay email complaintSame business day
VIP material complaintManager acknowledgement same day
AGHG-HCR-006

Historical Case Resolution Guide

Past resolution patterns by incident type · v3.0

Past cases guide proportional judgment; they are not automatic rules. Consistency depends on verified responsibility, impact duration, guest relationship, and whether the failure was preventable.

PatternTypical decision
Verified serious cleanliness failurePartial to one-night adjustment
Known, unresolved maintenance defectOne affected night, or more if repeated
Minor issue corrected quicklyApology, small amenity, or points
VIP material disruptionManager involvement, tailored remedy
Unsupported high-value demandRespectful explanation, modest goodwill if warranted
AGHG-GRC-001

Guest Recovery & Compensation Policy

Severity model, evidence, and approval authority · v3.0

Guests may raise a compensation request for anything from a minor inconvenience up to a critical safety incident. The remedy must match verified impact, not just the guest's ask — and every remedy needs an approver within their authority limit.

Level 1 — minor inconvenienceApology, amenity, up to 10% of affected night
Level 2 — service failure10–30% of affected night, room move, voucher
Level 3 — material stay disruptionOne affected night refund, future upgrade, points
Level 4 — critical incidentManager escalation, potential full-stay adjustment

AGHG-HSK-003

Housekeeping SOP & Room Quality Manual

Cleaning, inspection, and room status standards · v3.0

A room may only be released as "inspected" once it meets Aurelia's cleanliness and safety standard. Bathroom hygiene failures are treated more seriously than cosmetic ones, since they affect guest trust directly.

AreaCounts as a failure when
ToiletHair, visible stain, odor, unclean base
Shower / bathHair in drain, soap scum, mold marking, standing water
Sink & counterSpots, residue around tap, water rings
Towels & amenitiesMissing, stained, or unsealed items
AGHG-LVP-002

Loyalty & VIP Handling Policy

Tier recognition and recovery escalation · v3.0

Loyalty tier never proves a claim, but it changes urgency and who should be involved once impact is verified. High-value guests should never be passed between departments without a named owner.

SituationTarget time
VIP complaint during stay15-minute acknowledgement
VIP complaint after departureSame business day
Ambassador-tier complaintSame day, or next morning if after hours
Corporate account complaintWithin one business day
AGHG-MNT-004

Maintenance Escalation Manual

Defect priority and room-blocking rules · v3.0

A guest's compensation request often traces back to a maintenance defect. Priority must match guest impact, not repair convenience — and a room with an unresolved P1/P2 defect should be blocked rather than sold.
